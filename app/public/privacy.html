1. Glossary

  • Blockchain: A mathematically secured consensus ledger such as Polkadot, Ethereum, or other decentralized validation mechanisms.
  • Transaction: A change to the data set through a new entry in the Blockchain.
  • Pallet: Code deployed on the Blockchain, including self-execution of Transactions.
  • Token: A digital asset transferred in a Transaction, including DOT, ERC20, or other network tokens.
  • Wallet: A cryptographic storage solution permitting storage and transfer of tokens by correlation of Public and Private Keys, or Smart Contracts.
  • Private Key / Recovery Phrase: Unique credentials required to authorize Transactions, which must remain under your sole control.
  • Public Key: A sequence of numbers and letters identifying network participants on a Blockchain.
  • Profile: A user-provided, human-readable label linked to a Wallet or Account, stored locally on the device.

2. Blockchain and Privacy Limitations

Blockchains are immutable, public, and decentralized. This means personal data written to the Blockchain cannot be altered or deleted. As a result, rights such as the GDPR “right to erasure” may not apply to Blockchain data.

If you want to ensure your privacy rights are not impacted, you should not transact on Blockchains. Any data you write, including Wallet addresses, will become public and permanent.
